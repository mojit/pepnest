Vinyasa

Breath

The breathing style used in Ashtanga Vinyasa Yoga is referred to as "free breathing with sound" or "normal breath with free flow". This breathing is characterized by a relaxed diaphragmatic style, producing an ocean sound, which resonates in the practitioner's throat.

Bandhas

Another major principle of Ashtanga Vinyasa Yoga is the bandha, or muscle locking/contraction, which focuses energy in the body and is closely tied to the breath. There are a variety of bandhas.

Drishti

Dristhi is where you look while in the asana. In the ashtanga yoga method, there is a prescribed point of focus for every asana.
